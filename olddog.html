Old Dog

Thomas Stubblefield

They say "you cannot teach an old dog new tricks". The "They" in question are old lazy dogs who don't want to expend the effort to learn new tricks.

Young people have a monopoly on being beginners. Yet, for most, to be young is a choice.

Pen and paper, these are the tools of any true student. Such tools are also within reach to any so-called sage.

To change one's identity from that of an old dog sage to that of a student puppy is turning orange juice back into oranges.

To repair one's mind to it's original state, one must take not only the juice but the pulp it disgarded in the process.

To have the mind be a fruit, you must have not only the juice, the free flowly easily non-resistance work but also the bitter pulp.

Such a process is tedious and may take a lifetime in pursuit (spreading seeds all along the way for more fruits to blossom), but what's a better than to be an orange and remember once again what it's like to not be such an old damn dog.

~Thomas
